I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas is a live CD/DVD by American R&B recording artist, Beyoncé Knowles. It was filmed at the Encore Theater, Las Vegas by Ed Burke, on August 2, 2009, during a stint of Knowles' worldwide I Am... Tour (2009–2010). It features performances of over 30 songs, including her solo material, her recordings with the girl group Destiny's Child as well as behind-the-scenes footage. The album was made available in a DVD and a 2 CD/DVD deluxe format on November 23, 2009, and in a Blu-ray format on December 15, 2009. It was directed by Nick Wickham and produced by Emer Patten.

Footage from the DVD titled Beyoncé – I Am... Yours, aired on the American Broadcasting Company (ABC) on November 26, 2009. The special featured behind-the-scenes footage of Knowles' I Am... Tour and it was watched by over 5.2 million viewers. The album was also promoted by being broadcast on Channel 4 in the UK and re-released in a form of a live instrumentals album. Prior to its release, the album was placed on several lists of best DVDs and music videos for 2009. Upon its release, I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas received positive reviews from music critics, who generally praised the acoustic live performances of the songs. Knowles' vocals, the intimate performances and the narrating story of the album were also complimented.

The live version of "Halo" (2009), which is featured on the album, was nominated for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance at the 53rd Grammy Awards. The album stayed at the top of the US Billboard Top Music Video chart for eight weeks, and was certified double-platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA), denoting shipment of 200,000 copies. It became Knowles' second number one DVD in the US and it has spent fifty two weeks on the chart. The album was also successful on the other DVD and albums charts across the world and was certified platinum by the Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) for selling over 15,000 copies.

[edit] Background and filming

Main article: I Am... Yours
Knowles covered "I Wanna Be Where You Are" by Michael Jackson (pictured) during the revue.

On August 2, 2009, Knowles had a revue at the Encore Theater, Wynn Las Vegas on August 2, 2009, during a stint on her worldwide I Am... Tour (2009–2010). The revue was titled I Am... Yours. The show begins with a narrator introducing Knowles to the audience. She appears from the back of the theater performing "Hello".[1] She greets the audience and makes her way towards the stage as she continues to perform. At the stage, Knowles sings "Halo". Next, she performs "Irreplaceable" and interacts with the audience.[1] She later moves into an acoustic medley, performing a down-tempo version of "Sweet Dreams",[2] "Dangerously in Love 2" and "Sweet Love" (originally recorded by Anita Baker).[1] The medley leads into "If I Were a Boy" (which contains excerpts from "You Oughta Know") and "Scared of Lonely". The show continues with a performance of "That's Why You're Beautiful", intertwined with excerpts from "The Beautiful Ones" (originally recorded by Prince), "Satellites", and "Resentment". The first act concludes with Knowles performing a jazz-fused "Déjà Vu". Before the next act begins, three dancers appear and perform a tap sequence called "Bebop".[3]

Knowles begins the second act by telling the story of her career, starting from when she was nine years old to her then-latest album I Am... Sasha Fierce (2008). Knowles highlights one of the very first songs she remembers learning was Michael Jackson's "I Wanna Be Where You Are".[1] She continues with a medley of Destiny's Child's hits songs, including "No No No", "Bug a Boo", "Bills, Bills, Bills", and "Say My Name" whilst in between the songs, she talks about the story behind them. The medley leads into "Work It Out" and "'03 Bonnie & Clyde, as she now begins to talk about the start her solo career. As the set continues, Knowles explains how her record company felt her debut album did not have "one" hit song. She sarcastically remarks, "I guess they were kinda right... I had five."[1] Knowles goes on to perform "Crazy in Love" (reminiscent of "Proud Mary" by Tina Turner), "Naughty Girl" and "Get Me Bodied". As the show draws to a close, Knowles performs "Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)"; she then exits the stage after thanking the audience for their presence.[3]

[edit] Development

I Am... Yours was filmed at the Encore Theater in Wynn Las Vegas

After Knowles finished her I Am... Yours revue, several publications reported that a live album containing the performance of the revue will be released.[4][5] In early November 2009, it was announced that she would be releasing her first concert performance DVD and live CD titled I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas.[6][7] The DVD features performances from the I Am... Yours revue. It contains performances of over 30 songs, including Knowles' solo material as well as her recordings with the girl group Destiny's Child.[6][7] Exclusive behind-the-scenes footage was also placed on the album;[6][7][8] part of which was later added on Knowles' 2011 video album Live at Roseland: Elements of 4.[9] According to Music World Entertainment president and CEO, Mathew Knowles, the decision to release I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas was prompted by the way the music was presented in the digital world and because "the live experience is becoming more important".[10] He added that the live performances show the ability of the artists to entertain live, something that according to him, "Knowles has proven that time after time".[10] The album was directed by Nick Wickham and produced by Emer Patten.[11] It was filmed by Ed Burke and executively produced by Beyoncé Knowles, Mathew Knowles and Sheira Rees-Davies.[11][12][13] Knowles' performance is backed by her all-female band, Suga Mama and an orchestra.[12] The show was choreographed by Knowles and Frank Gatson Jr.[14] Regarding her I Am... Yours revue, Knowles said,

"I've always wanted to perform in Las Vegas, I've always wanted to do my own show there, because a lot of the icons that I look up to had amazing shows in Las Vegas. I thought if I'm going to have my own Las Vegas show, it has to be right and it has to have heart and soul and it has to be something memorable... My whole objective here is for people to see what they don't get to see. Just give me my band, give me a stage, some cool smoke and lights and you see the sweat, you see the pain, you see the love, you see the soul and it's about music."[15]

[edit] Release and promotion

The album was available in a DVD and a 2 CD/DVD deluxe edition on November 23, 2009, and in a Blu-ray format on December 15, 2009.[6][7] The DVD and Blu-ray editions feature the entire concert (split into three parts), as well as over 23 minutes of behind-the-scenes footage, while the two CDs include only the audio from the main performances.[6][7] On November 26, 2009, ABC Network aired a one-hour television special called Beyoncé – I Am... Yours at 9:00 PM/EST.[6][15][16] A one-minute trailer of the special was posted online on November 12, 2009;[17][18] it showed Knowles' during her I Am... Tour, behind-the-scenes footage including rehearsals, as well as personal videos.[19][20] According to USA Today, the special was watched by 5.2 million viewers.[21] Entertainment Weekly's Brad Wete commented that she was "danc[ing] her tail off" during the special.[22] John Griffiths of Us Weekly reviewed the special positively, calling it "shimmery... mostly culled from her lavish concert".[23] He added that she further "dishes about her career, shows off her childhood home videos, belts out her hits and more."[23] The show was broadcast on Channel 4 in the UK on December 25, 2009.[24] A live instrumentals album of the songs performed on the revue was released at Amazon.com and the iTunes Store on September 28, 2010.[25][26] Several videos of the live performances of "Halo", "Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)" and "Scared of Lonely" were released at MTV Latin America.[27] These videos were used to promote the album in Latin America and the television special "MTV World Stage: Beyoncé Live from Las Vegas".[28]

Tanya Remekie of Rap-Up felt that the album contains a "show-stopping event as Sasha Fierce and Bey[oncé] come out to play".[1] She finished her review by writing that the "Broadway-fashioned narration of her life story, told through song and dance.. shares her magical story."[1] During a review of the album, the writers of People magazine rated the album with three-and-a-half out of four stars and wrote that it "offers nicely subdued versions of her hits".[32] They further described the show as a "magic".[32] Another writer of the same publication praised the album, writing that "You'll get to know Beyoncé like never before".[34] Similarly, a writer of Eye Weekly called the album "extraordinary" before adding that it is "the chance to experience her [Knowles] up close and personal".[35] A writer of Us Weekly said that to see Knowles' "fiereceness", people should listen to her album I Am... Sasha Fierce or buy the DVD I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas to see the live experience.[36] Allmusic's Andy Kellman awarded I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas a rating of four out of five stars, describing it as a "theatrical production worthy of Vegas, with Beyoncé and her large backing band energetically rolling [through Knowles' songs]".[29] He noted that "what makes I Am...Yours stand apart from a typical live-album cash-in is the mostly unexpected covers that are weaved into the set."[29] Kellman finished his review by writing, "As live albums go, this is not quite destined to be one of those all-time classics; a couple missing songs and a little too much talk aside, however, the fans couldn’t ask for more."[29]

Ed Masley of The Arizona Republic praised the intimacy of the concert and the acoustic versions of the songs.[37] A writer of The Boston Globe also gave I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas a positive review, comparing it with Sin City. He commended the jazz interlude, the acoustic rearrangements of the songs, and the "charming B[eyoncé] telling her life story in [the] song[s]".[38] Darryl Sterdan of the website Jam! also compared the album with Sin City and commented: "Only Beyonce would think an 'intimate' theatre gig should include her massive all-girl band, an orchestra, lasers, dancers, costume changes and a wind machine to keep her hair blowing majestically. And only Beyonce could pull it all together as superbly as she does in this 98-minute... show".[31] Grading I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas with an A- and calling it the "best live concert film of the year", David Burger of The Salt Lake Tribune concluded, "Instead of a traditional retread of her hits, she narrates and emotionally sings her way through a souful musical journey that takes her from her first audition song to the mega-hits she unleashed this past year... This is a thrilling tour de force."[33]

Likewise, Mike Diver of BBC Online reviewed the album positively, praising the narrating story of the performances calling it "polished, [and] professional".[30] Diver further noted that Knowles' vocals "peak and dip, tremble and roar, mercifully without ever hitting Mariah Carey levels of skull-rattling intensity".[30] He added that the album would look predictable because it was an "entirely second-guessable affair with each movement telegraphed and every realigned arrangement (bombast turned down, jazz and funk switched up) meeting the listener’s expectations head on".[30] The Huffington Post's Mike Ragogna compared Knowles with Diana Ross during the concert. He added, "Beyoncé's I Am...Yours is a satisfying decade-and-then-some retrospective that reveals the artist's interesting back story with a personal warmth".[12] Nate Chinen of The New York Times noted that the album documents a smaller show than the other ones Knowles usually makes. He further stated, "But the stagecraft — wind machines, lasers, arena lighting — keeps a viewer at a worshipful remove... Of course Beyoncé has always fared better with declaration than with disclosure, framing her sentiments as actions. Still, the old-fashioned extravagance of this production... feels like some kind of honesty: it's a sincere gesture from a show-business savant."[14]

[edit] Recognition

Before the release of I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas, Rap-Up listed it as one of the "most notable releases" for the Fall 2009.[39] Likewise, MTV News placed the album on its list of "Holiday Album Preview".[40] USA Today's Mike Snider also mentioned it on his list of "Music-video DVDs that make worthy gifts this season" comparing its acoustic style to the VH1 Storytellers series.[41] Jay Lustig of New Jersey On-Line listed I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas in two of his lists of most notable albums in the fourth quarter of 2009.[42][43] Mark Edward Nero of the website About.com put it in his list of "2009 Holiday Gift Guide for R&B Fans".[44] The live performance of "Halo" (2009), which is featured on the album, was nominated for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance at the 53rd Grammy Awards.[45][46] It charted at number fifty-eight on the Canadian Hot 100 chart.[47][48] On The Village Voice's 2009 Pazz & Jop albums list, the DVD was ranked at number 1643.[49]

[edit] Chart performance

I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas debuted at number one on the US Billboard Top Music Video, becoming Knowles' second number-one DVD in that country.[50][51][52] The following week, it fell to number two, but later rebounded to the top where it stayed for the next seven consecutive weeks.[51] It spent ten weeks inside the top five – eight of them at the top.[51] In 2009, the album sold 99,000 copies in the US, making it the sixth best-selling music DVD of the year.[53] As of December 29, 2010 it had sold 162,000 units in the US, and ranked at number fifteen on the DVD year-end chart of 2010.[53] It additionally became the best-selling music video of 2010 in the US.[54] It was certified double-platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for shipping over 200,000 copies.[55] I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas spent a total of fifty two weeks on the top of the chart.[51]

Outside the US, I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas peaked at number eleven on the Australian DVD Chart and on the Australian Urban Albums Chart.[56][57] It was certified platinum by the Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA), denoting sales of over 15,000 copies.[58] It became the forty-fourth best-selling DVD of 2010 in Australia.[59] On the New Zealand Music DVD Chart, the album peaked at number six.[60] I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas debuted at number twenty four on the UK R&B Albums Chart on December 19, 2009.[61] The next week it fell at number thirty one,[62] and it was last seen in the top forty of the chart on January 2, 2010, at number thirty three.[63]
