I Am... Yours was a revue show by American R&B recording artist Beyoncé Knowles. It was held four-consecutive nights in July and August, 2009 in support of her third studio album I Am... Sasha Fierce (2008). The concerts were held at the Encore Theatre in Las Vegas, Nevada as part of her worldwide I Am... Tour (2009-10). Knowles performed over thirty songs backed by an orchestra and her all-female band, Suga Mama to a standing room-only audience of 1,500. The show was deemed "an intimate encounter" as Knowles portrayed a more raw and uninhibited show versus her previous concert performances. The concept of the shows revolves around Knowles' recording career.

I Am... Yours received positive response from music critics who praised the intimate performances and Knowles' vocals as well as the jazz, funk and orchestral arrangements of the songs. The success of I Am... Yours has led Wynn Resorts to ask Knowles to return as a residency act. A double disc CD and DVD I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas was released in November 2009, featuring the performance recorded on August 2, 2009. It contained the full concert, a behind-the-scenes feature, along with an audio CD of the concert. I Am... Yours was aired on several TV Networks as a special.

[edit] Background and development

The revue was held at the Encore Theater in Wynn Las Vegas to a standing-audience.

When Knowles' I Am... Tour (2009-2010), was announced in 2008 by Entertainment Weekly, the article mentioned Knowles would perform a week-long Las Vegas showcase.[1] As tour dates were announced, it was later revealed that Knowles would perform four shows in Las Vegas. According to Knowles, the show is an "unplugged" version of her normal concerts; she removed all the elaborate costumes and theatrics of her latest tour and performed with her band, Suga Mama, and a small team of dancers.[2][3] The concerts were held at the Encore Theatre in Las Vegas, Nevada to a standing room-only audience of 1,500.[4] The set list included performances of over thirty songs.[5] The show was choreographed by Frank Jr. Gatson.[6] Regarding the revue, Knowles told ABC News,

"I've always wanted to perform in Las Vegas, I've always wanted to do my own show there, because a lot of the icons that I look up to had amazing shows in Las Vegas. I thought if I'm going to have my own Las Vegas show, it has to be right and it has to have heart and soul and it has to be something memorable... My whole objective here is for people to see what they don't get to see. Just give me my band, give me a stage, some cool smoke and lights and you see the sweat, you see the pain, you see the love, you see the soul and it's about music."[7]

During the documentary, "What Happens in Vegas...", Knowles stated that when the idea of a Vegas show was presented to her, she wanted the show to be a complete 180 from her current tour. Also, she did not want it to be the typical Las Vegas revue showcase. She wanted to remove the theatrics and "consciously show that I don't need any of that stuff. Just give me my band, a stage and some cool smoke and lights."[8] Knowles and her production quickly organized the show in seven days, based on a script provided by filmmaker Matthew Dickens.[8] She often rehearsed during sound checks of her 2009 tour.[9]

The concept of the shows revolves around Knowles' recording career, from her early childhood beginnings to the star she is today. She revealed, "In the beginning, I'm gonna start out, sing and make it about my songwriting, my arrangements, my band and my love for music. Then, I'm gonna tell my story. This way, I can sing all of the hit songs, the songs that people want. But, it's a deeper meaning... these songs meant more than just the chorus that's catchy... it's my life."[8] Since the show was created last minute, there were many changes. Originally, the set list included, "Ego", "At Last", "Listen", "Ring the Alarm", and "Upgrade U".[8] These songs were later removed from the set list and a new one was added with additional instruments.[8][9] The success of the show has led Wynn Resorts to ask Knowles to return as a residency act.[10][11][12]

[edit] About the show

[edit] Fashion and stage

The stage contained wind machines, lasers, arena lighting and a retro, mood-lit platform during the performances of the song.[6][9] According to Nate Chinen of The New York Times, "the stagecraft... keeps a viewer at a worshipful remove".[6] During a statement before the show, Knowles revealed, "We have designed an elegant and beautiful stage and we will have new costumes that are being designed especially for this show."[9] Knowles is accompanied by an orchestra[3] and her all-female band on stage "whose satin interpretations pump lush over pomp" according to Mike Ragogna of The Huffington Post.[2] He further compared their wardrobe with Thierry Mugler's designs.[2] She is also backed by several dancers.[3] Several costumes for the stage during the show were designed by Beyoncé's mother Tina Knowles, who was her designer for many years.[13]

[edit] Show synopsis

The show begins with a narrator introducing Knowles to the audience. She appears from the back of the theater performing "Hello".[2][14] She greets the audience and makes her way towards the stage as she continues to perform. At the stage, Knowles sings "Halo". Next, she performs "Irreplaceable" and interacts with the audience.[14] She later moves into an acoustic medley, performing a down-tempo version of "Sweet Dreams",[15] "Dangerously in Love 2" and "Sweet Love" (originally recorded by Anita Baker).[14] The medley leads into "If I Were a Boy" (which contains excerpts from "You Oughta Know") and "Scared of Lonely". The show continues with a performance of "That's Why You're Beautiful", intertwined with excerpts from "The Beautiful Ones" (originally recorded by Prince), "Satellites", and "Resentment".[16] The first act concludes with Knowles performing a jazz-fused "Déjà Vu". Before the next act begins, three dancers appear and perform a tap sequence called "Bebop".[16]

Knowles begins the second act by telling the story of her career, starting from when she was nine years old to her then-latest album I Am... Sasha Fierce (2008).[16] Knowles highlights that one of the very first songs she remembers learning was Michael Jackson's "I Wanna Be Where You Are".[14] She continues with a medley of Destiny's Child's hits songs, including "No No No", "Bug a Boo", "Bills, Bills, Bills", and "Say My Name" whilst in between the songs, she talks about the story behind them. The medley leads into "Work It Out" and "'03 Bonnie & Clyde", as she now begins to talk about the start her solo career. As the set continues, Knowles explains how her record company felt her debut album did not have "one" hit song. She sarcastically remarks, "I guess they were kinda right... I had five."[14] Knowles goes on to perform "Crazy in Love" (reminiscent of "Proud Mary" by Tina Turner), "Naughty Girl" and "Get Me Bodied".[16] As the show draws to a close, Knowles performs "Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)"; she then exits the stage after thanking the audience for their presence.[16][6]

[edit] Critical reception

"[...] beyond the customary thanks, introductions and shout-outs, Knowles' banter is thin on the ground – requests for the crowd to get up on their feet are answered, and the singer's patter is perfunctory rather than particularly tailored to this occasion. But this is a polished, professional performance... Knowles doesn't put a dangerously high heel wrong at any step. The stories that precede her cover versions are neatly scripted, succinct and fit the flow of the set well. Her vocals peak and dip, tremble and roar, mercifully without ever hitting Mariah Carey levels of skull-rattling intensity. All told, all boxes ticked.

— Mike Diver, BBC Online[17]

Overall, the show received a positive response from many music critics. Mike Weatherford of the Las Vegas Review-Journal wrote, "The spectacle rivals Celine Dion's 'A New Day' as the singer transforms from fragile soul to the rock warrior alter ego of her album title, I Am ... Sasha Fierce."[9] John Griffiths of Us Weekly described the concert as "lavish".[18] Andy Kellman from Allmusic wrote, "The show is certainly a theatrical production worthy of Vegas, with Beyoncé and her large backing band energetically rolling through the singer's back catalog."[19] Darryl Sterdan of the website Jam! commented: "Only Beyonce would think an 'intimate' theatre gig should include her massive all-girl band, an orchestra, lasers, dancers, costume changes and a wind machine to keep her hair blowing majestically. And only Beyonce could pull it all together as superbly as she does in this 98-minute... show".[3]

A writer of The Boston Globe praised I Am... Yours, comparing it with Sin City. He commended the jazz interlude, the acoustic rearrangements of the songs, and the "charming B[eyoncé] telling her life story in [the] song[s]".[20] Mike Ragogna of The Huffington Post compared Knowles with Diana Ross during the show and added: "This 'concert' is a staged, stripped-down reconfiguration by Beyoncé... of her current touring extravaganza that covers her biggest hits... as she whisks us through her career highlights in two tidy acts plus an 'intermission' that is nothing of the sort (it's a jazz medley with a dance payoff)."[2] He finished his review by saying, "Beyoncé's I Am...Yours is a satisfying decade-and-then-some retrospective that reveals the artist's interesting back story with a personal warmth".[2]

Mike Diver of BBC Online reviewed the revue positively saying, "this is an entirely second-guessable affair, each movement telegraphed and every realigned arrangement (bombast turned down, jazz and funk switched up) meeting the listener's expectations head on."[17] The writer of People magazine described the show as a "magic".[21] Nate Chinen of The New York Times positively noted that I Am... Yours documents a smaller show than the other ones Knowles usually makes.[6] A less favorable review came from Margaret Lyons of Entertainment Weekly. She writes, "I just wish this special had the balls to call itself 'I Am…Mine' and admit that among the many things we admire about Beyonce is that she's not ours. In fact the opposite’s true. We're hers".[22]

[edit] Broadcasts and recordings

Main article: I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas

A double disc CD and DVD, titled I Am... Yours: An Intimate Performance at Wynn Las Vegas, was released in November 2009, featuring the performance recorded on August 2, 2009.[5] The collection featured the full concert, a behind-the-scenes feature, along with an audio CD of the concert.[23][24][25][26] The collection was very successful, debuting at number 1 on the US Billboard Top Music Video chart.[27][28] The album was also successful on the other DVD and albums charts across the world. ABC Network aired a one-hour television special called Beyoncé – I Am... Yours on November 26, 2009, as a Thanksgiving special.[5][7][29] A one-minute trailer of the special was posted online on November 12, 2009;[30][31] it showed Knowles' during her I Am... Tour, behind-the-scenes footage including rehearsals, as well as personal videos.[32][33] The special featured various songs from the show, as well as backstage footage, showing Knowles and her team preparing for the shows.[18] The show was a success, drawing in nearly five million viewers.[34][35] The live performance of "Halo" during the show, which is featured on the album, was nominated for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance at the 53rd Grammy Awards.[36][37]

Black Entertainment Television aired the entire concert including songs that were not featured on the ABC special.[38] In the United Kingdom, I Am... Yours was broadcast on Channel 4 on December 25, 2009, (Christmas Day morning),[39] following an hour-long documentary named Beyoncé: For The Record. Several videos of the live performances of "Halo", "Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)" and "Scared of Lonely" were released at MTV Latin America in 2010 and 2011.[40] These videos were used to promote the album in Latin America and the television special "MTV World Stage: Beyoncé Live from Las Vegas".[41]
