List of Hot 100 number-one singles of 2012 (U.S.)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search
Country pop singer Taylor Swift scored her first number one single with "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together" which became the highest first week single sales by any other female artist, surpassing Lady Gaga's "Born This Way" and Kesha's "Tik Tok" as the largest single week sales by any other female artist.

The Billboard Hot 100 is a chart that ranks the best-performing singles of the United States. Its data, published by Billboard magazine and compiled by Nielsen SoundScan, is based collectively on each single's weekly physical and digital sales, as well as airplay. In 2012, 13 singles claimed the top spot in 52 issues of the magazine, one of which, singer Rihanna's "We Found Love" spent eight weeks at the peak position in late 2011.[1]

In 2012, six acts achieved their first US number-one single, either as a lead artist or a featured guest: Fun, Janelle Monáe, Gotye, Kimbra, Carly Rae Jepsen and Taylor Swift. Three collaboration singles topped the chart. Pop singer Rihanna was the only artist to achieve two number-one singles ("Diamonds" and "We Found Love"). Gotye's "Somebody That I Used to Know" became the year's biggest-selling single, topping the Billboard Year-End Hot 100.

Carly Rae Jepsen and Maroon 5 tied for the longest-running number one single of 2012 in the US, spending nine consecutive weeks each with their singles "Call Me Maybe" and "One More Night". This marked Jepsen's first number-one single in the US and Maroon 5's third. Gotye's "Somebody That I Used to Know" spent a total of eight weeks atop the Billboard Hot 100. Both Fun.'s "We Are Young (featuring Janelle Monáe) and Bruno Mars' Locked Out of Heaven (4 weeks in 2013), spent a total of six weeks on top, while Rihanna, Kelly Clarkson and Taylor Swift tied with three weeks atop the Billboard Hot 100.

One song debuted at the top of the chart in 2012: Katy Perry's "Part of Me", becoming the twentieth single in the chart's 54-year history to debut at number one.

Chart history

Indie rock band, Fun, scored its first number-one single with "We Are Young", which also peaked at number one on four other U.S. Billboard charts.
Carly Rae Jepsen's "Call Me Maybe" tied for the most weeks at number one with Maroon 5's "One More Night" at nine weeks. "Call Me Maybe" is the second best-selling song of the year with over 6 million digital copies sold.
Adele's "Set Fire to the Rain" made her the only British female artist in history to have three consecutive No. 1s on the chart.
Rihanna's "Diamonds" is her 12th No. 1 on the chart, tying her with Madonna and The Supremes.
